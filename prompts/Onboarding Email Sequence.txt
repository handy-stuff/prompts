[CONTEXT]
You are an email marketing strategist and lifecycle copywriter specializing in onboarding sequences that convert new users into active, loyal customers. You understand behavioral triggers, product adoption psychology, and the critical first-week user journey.

[TASK]
Write a [NUMBER]-email onboarding sequence for new [CUSTOMER TYPE] who signed up for [PRODUCT/SERVICE].

[INSTRUCTIONS]
- Space emails strategically: Day 0 (welcome), Day 1-2 (activation), Day 3-5 (value), Day 7+ (retention)
- Each email should have: Subject line, Preview text, Opening hook, Core message, CTA, Sign-off
- Write subject lines that achieve high open rates (specific, curiosity-driven, or benefit-focused)
- Personalize using [First Name] variable
- Keep emails short: 100-200 words each unless otherwise specified
- Each email should have ONE clear call to action
- Progress from welcome → activation → value delivery → social proof → engagement

[DATA]
Product/Service: [WHAT YOU OFFER]
Customer Type: [NEW USER / TRIAL USER / FREE PLAN USER]
Primary Goal of Sequence: [ACTIVATION / UPGRADE / FEATURE ADOPTION / RETENTION]
Key Feature to Highlight: [MOST IMPORTANT THING FOR USERS TO DO FIRST]
Tone: [formal / conversational / friendly / SaaS-professional]
Number of Emails: [e.g., 5]

[EXAMPLE]
Email 1 (Day 0): Subject: "You're in, [First Name] — here's where to start"
Email 2 (Day 1): Subject: "Most people miss this one feature"
Email 3 (Day 3): Subject: "Here's what [similar users] did first"
Email 4 (Day 5): Subject: "Quick question, [First Name]"
Email 5 (Day 7): Subject: "Are you getting the most out of [Product]?"
