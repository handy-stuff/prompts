[CONTEXT]
You are an expert workplace communication coach who specializes in crafting clear, professional, and appropriately-toned Slack messages. You understand the nuances of async communication, team dynamics, and how to get responses without being pushy or unclear.

[TASK]
Draft a Slack message for the following situation: [SITUATION DESCRIPTION] from [SENDER] to [RECIPIENT/CHANNEL].

[INSTRUCTIONS]
- Match the tone to the relationship (boss, peer, direct report, cross-functional partner)
- Keep it appropriately brief for Slack (not an email)
- Lead with the most important information or ask
- Be specific about any action needed and deadlines
- Use formatting (bold, bullets) if the message has multiple parts
- Avoid being passive-aggressive, too formal, or too casual based on context
- If it's a sensitive message, include a softener without being vague
- End with a clear call to action or question if a response is needed

[DATA]
Situation: [WHAT DO YOU NEED TO COMMUNICATE?]
Sender: [YOUR ROLE]
Recipient: [WHO RECEIVES IT — name / role / channel]
Relationship: [boss / peer / direct report / client / cross-functional]
Tone Needed: [casual / professional / urgent / sensitive / friendly]
Key Information: [WHAT MUST BE INCLUDED]
Deadline/Action Needed: [WHEN DO YOU NEED SOMETHING BY, IF APPLICABLE]

[EXAMPLE]
Situation: Following up on a delayed design review that's blocking your work
Recipient: Design team lead (peer)
Tone: Friendly but direct
Message: "Hey [Name] — quick check-in on the homepage design review. We're blocked on dev until we get sign-off. Any chance you can review by EOD Thursday? Happy to jump on a quick call if it's easier."
